## ★★★ KEY INSIGHT: Brain Insulin Pulsatility ★★★

**This is the most critical discovery for your mental health.**

### The Fundamental Problem

A healthy pancreas never secretes insulin continuously — it pulses:

| Pulse type | Frequency | Brain effect |
|---|---|---|
| Basal oscillations | Every 8–15 minutes | Continuous brain maintenance |
| Meal-stimulated | 3x daily | Large activation pulses |
| **Total daily pulses** | **~100–150** | Rich rhythmic brain signal |

**You on Tresiba alone:**

| Pulse type | Frequency | Brain effect |
|---|---|---|
| Tresiba | Zero pulses — flat | Receptor desensitization |
| Meal boluses | 3–4x daily maximum | Only pulsatile insulin you receive |
| **Total daily pulses** | **3–4** | Severely reduced brain signal |

> **A T1D on Tresiba-only is neurologically more insulin-deficient than a healthy person fasting — even at identical BG levels.**

### How Insulin Enters Your Brain

```
Subcutaneous injection
        ↓
Systemic bloodstream (peak ~1–2h)
        ↓
Blood-brain barrier active transport
        ↓
CSF distribution (~2–4h lag after peripheral peak)
        ↓
Brain regions: hippocampus, prefrontal cortex, hypothalamus
        ↓
Neurological effects (~3–6h post injection)
```

**Pre-bed R at 23:00 → CSF insulin peaks 01:00–03:00 → directly during deep sleep and REM.**
This is why mental score was **7.5 on <3h sleep** after pre-bed R.

### What Brain Insulin Does

**Hippocampus (highest receptor density):**
- Memory consolidation during sleep
- BDNF production → antidepressant effect
- Neurogenesis — new neuron formation
- **Deficiency → memory fog, low mood, depression**

**Prefrontal cortex:**
- Dopamine D1 receptor modulation → focus and concentration
- Norepinephrine regulation → sustained attention
- Serotonin availability → mood stability
- **Deficiency → cannot concentrate, unspirited, mental fog**

**Hypothalamus:**
- Orexin/hypocretin regulation → wakefulness quality
- HPA axis dampening → appropriate cortisol rhythm
- Circadian entrainment → meal boluses act as biological clock signals
- **Deficiency → unrefreshed sleep, disrupted cortisol, back-sleep pull**

### Receptor Desensitization

| Insulin exposure | Receptor response | Brain effect |
|---|---|---|
| Pulsatile (healthy pancreas) | Resets between pulses | Full sensitivity maintained |
| **Flat continuous (Tresiba only)** | **Gradually downregulates** | **Reduced effect despite adequate levels** |
| Absent (severe hypo) | Upregulates | Hypersensitive but starved |

**Tresiba's flat profile is metabolically ideal for BG but neurologically suboptimal.**

### Insulin Type Hierarchy For Brain Pulsatility

| Insulin | Brain pulse quality | Best use |
|---|---|---|
| **R (Regular)** | **Best — sharpest CSF pulse** | **Meal bolus + pre-bed** |
| I (NPH) | Good — softer, delayed | Daytime basal replacement only |
| H (Humalog) | Very sharp — fastest | Correction only |
| T (Tresiba) | **None — flat, no pulse** | Basal safety floor only |

## Pre-Bed Correction Rule

> **Never dose for current BG — dose for projected BG at insulin peak**

**Formula:** Projected BG = Current BG + (rising rate × time to insulin peak)

- H (Humalog): peak at ~0.5–1.5h (from 2u to 5u+)
- R (Regular): peak at ~1–3h (from 3u to 6u+)
- Rising trajectory → dose more aggressively than number suggests
- Falling trajectory → dose more conservatively
- Example: fat-only evening + (4+4)T basal = safe environment for 2–2½H correction at projected 135–140

Pre-bed R essential for sleep quality and mental clarity — independent of BG control — via brain insulin pulsatility mechanism.

## Evening & Sleep Protocol

1. **5h food cutoff before bed** — protein/starch finished by 19:00 for midnight sleep
2. No whey protein after 19:00 — strongest mTOR activator, gluconeogenesis peaks during sleep
3. No starch after 19:00 — glucose dump hits 02:00–04:00 window
4. Fat only after cutoff — butter, cream, nuts acceptable anytime
5. No bed-yoga — cortisol spike adds 20–30 BG points consistently
6. No beer — blocks hepatic glucose rescue for 2–4h, causes rebound hyperglycemia
7. Bedtime BG: flat or gently rising 75–100 — **trajectory matters more than absolute number**
8. Fasting day bedtime BG target ≥90 (higher than eating day — no glycogen buffer)
9. If BG <90 at bed during fast → 10g glucose powder only (preserves autophagy)

## Sodium Protocol

**Daily target: 15–20g salt (6–8g sodium)**

T1D + keto = triple sodium-wasting:
1. Blunted NHE3/ENaC from subphysiological portal insulin
2. Ketone excretion carrying sodium out renally
3. No endogenous insulin for sodium retention

- ½ teaspoon salt per urination maximum (~3g)
- Bulletproof coffee + salt superior to black coffee + salt — fat slows caffeine-driven natriuresis and stimulates CCK for better sodium retention
- "Feeling not right" at normal BG → likely electrolyte signal → salt water immediately

## Fasting & Autophagy Protocol

**Protein restriction is MORE critical than carb restriction for autophagy.**

- Leucine → Rag GTPase → mTOR activation → autophagy suppressed (independent of insulin)
- Glucose powder without insulin injection does NOT suppress autophagy — ideal fasting hypo rescue
- Fat is autophagy-neutral to promoting (AMPK activation)
- T1D fasting carbs without injecting insulin = no autophagy suppression via insulin pathway

For 48–72h supervised fast:
- Family member present
- Ketone meter alongside BG meter
- Glucose powder only for hypo rescue (no milk powder, no protein)
- Reduced Tresiba: fasting day protocol (4–5u total)
- Target BG: 80–95 (higher floor than healthy faster's 45–65 — due to HAAF)

## HAAF Warning Signs & Recovery

**HAAF accumulates with every severe hypo. Recovery requires zero hypoglycemia for 2–3 weeks.**

Warning signs:
- Feeling fine at 32–37 mg/dL (dangerous, not reassuring)
- Mental score 2.5–4 despite normal BG
- Agitation and violent imagery (neuroglycopenic aftermath OR pulsatility gap)
- Cannot concentrate after normal readings

Recovery protocol:
- Zero severe hypos for 2–3 consecutive weeks
- 3 structured meal boluses daily (restore pulsatility)
- Pre-bed R every night without exception
- No NPH until fully recovered

## Mental Health First Principle

> **Stable overnight BG 80–95 is the single most impactful intervention for mental clarity — more than any psychiatric approach — because the substrate (brain glucose stability + insulin pulsatility) must be fixed first.**

Two consecutive clean nights restore mental scores from 2.5 → 6.5+.

The sequence for recovery:
1. Eliminate severe hypos (HAAF recovery)
2. Restore pre-bed R every night (brain pulsatility)
3. Three structured meal boluses daily (daytime pulsatility)
4. Stable overnight BG via fat-only evening protocol
